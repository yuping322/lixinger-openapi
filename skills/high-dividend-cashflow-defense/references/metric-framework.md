# High Dividend Defensive Enhancement - Metric Framework

## 1) Dividend Sustainability (P1, 30)

- Dividend yield level: high but not extreme outlier.
- Dividend continuity: years of uninterrupted payment.
- Dividend payout ratio (profit-based): avoid structurally >80% without strong utility-like stability.
- Dividend growth consistency: stable/gradual growth preferred over cliff-like pattern.

Suggested scoring:
- 25-30: stable history + prudent payout
- 15-24: moderate volatility or short history
- 0-14: unstable payouts, repeated cuts, or overly aggressive payout

## 2) Cash Flow Quality (P2, 35)

Core anti-trap pillar.

- CFO margin trend
- FCF trend
- FCF/Dividends coverage ratio
- Cash conversion (CFO / net profit)
- Working-capital driven distortions

Hard warning patterns:
- High dividend + weak/negative FCF for multiple periods
- Persistent mismatch between accounting profit and operating cash
- Dividend funded by financing activities rather than operating surplus

## 3) Balance Sheet Resilience (P3, 20)

- Net debt/EBITDA trend
- Interest coverage trend
- Current ratio / quick ratio
- Debt maturity profile (short-term pressure)
- Cash & equivalents buffer

Hard warning patterns:
- Short-term debt rising while cash buffer shrinking
- Debt refinancing dependency increases but payout unchanged

## 4) Valuation Margin (P4, 15)

- Relative valuation vs own history (PE/PB/EV-EBITDA percentile)
- Relative valuation vs sector peers
- Implied earnings/FCF yield vs bond yield spread

Rule of thumb:
- Good company + expensive valuation can still underperform.
- Prefer acceptable quality at fair-to-cheap valuation range.

## 5) Red-Flag Checklist (Immediate Trap Candidate)

If 2+ conditions are true, treat as high-risk trap unless disproven:

- Dividend yield spikes after price collapse without cash flow support.
- Consecutive periods of weak FCF coverage (<1.0).
- Significant debt increase while maintaining or increasing payout.
- One-off gains support earnings while core cash generation weakens.
- Management guidance signals caution but dividend policy not adjusted.

## 6) Rebalancing Triggers

Downgrade or remove when any occurs:
- Dividend cut/suspension.
- FCF coverage drops below threshold for 2 consecutive periods.
- Sharp deterioration in debt service indicators.
- Valuation rerates to expensive zone with no quality improvement.
