### 3) Interpretation

#### 3.1 Execution quality

- Is the company actually using the authorization, or is it mostly signaling.
- Has buyback spend translated into lower diluted share count.
- Are repurchases happening at reasonable valuation levels.

#### 3.2 Sustainability

- Is buyback activity funded by free cash flow or balance-sheet expansion.
- How cyclical is the company’s buyback capacity.
- Does management have a consistent return policy across cycles.

#### 3.3 What matters for investors

- When buybacks deserve a premium as a capital-allocation signal.
- When buybacks are cosmetic and mainly offset SBC dilution.
- What would make the current buyback thesis materially weaker.

### 4) Risks and Monitoring

| Risk | Trigger | Monitor | Mitigation / what changes the view |
