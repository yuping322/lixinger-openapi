### 3) Interpretation

#### 3.1 Why rebalancing now

- Explain the trigger clearly and whether it is structural or temporary.
- State whether the goal is risk control, style reset, or tactical adjustment.
- Clarify what would happen if no rebalance were done.

#### 3.2 Portfolio impact

- How sector, factor, and single-name concentration will change.
- Whether the portfolio becomes more defensive, more balanced, or more directional.
- Whether some intentional active tilts should be preserved.

#### 3.3 Execution plan

- Whether to execute immediately or in tranches.
- How to manage cost, liquidity, and timing risk.
- Which trades are mandatory vs optional.

### 4) Risks and Monitoring

| Risk | Trigger | Monitor | Mitigation / what changes the view |
|---|---|---|---|
| Over-trading | Small drifts cause too many trades | Turnover, cost | Set minimum thresholds |
| Style reversal after rebalance | Market rotates quickly | Factor returns, breadth | Use staged execution |
| Liquidity / slippage | Trade sizes too large | ADV %, spread, slippage | Extend execution window |
| Target is unstable | Allocation assumptions change | Risk budget, benchmark | Reconfirm target before execution |

### 5) Next Steps

- Verify cost, liquidity, and order-size assumptions.
- Create a post-trade review of exposures and tracking error.
- Recheck whether tax-aware adjustments should modify the trade list.

### 6) Data Gaps / Confidence

- Missing data: exact realized tax lot details, real-time execution conditions.
- Proxy used: current weights, target weights, ADV/liquidity, and historical cost assumptions.
- Confidence level: Medium.
