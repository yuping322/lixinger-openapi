### 3) Interpretation

#### 3.1 Internal health of the market

- Is the rally being carried by a narrow group or broad participation.
- Are new highs consistent with the headline index.
- Is equal-weight confirming or contradicting cap-weight strength.

#### 3.2 What the divergence means

- Weak breadth at highs often signals fragility.
- Improving breadth under a flat index can signal rotation or early repair.
- Sector participation helps distinguish broad risk appetite from theme concentration.

#### 3.3 What matters next

- Which breadth metric is most important in the current regime.
- What would confirm continuation vs raise drawdown risk.
- How breadth should influence risk posture.

### 4) Risks and Monitoring

| Risk | Trigger | Monitor | Mitigation / what changes the view |
|---|---|---|---|
| Narrow leadership breaks | Mega-cap leaders falter | Equal-weight, sector breadth | Reduce dependence on narrow leaders |
| Breadth deterioration accelerates | New lows expand | New highs-lows, A/D line | Raise caution |
| False improvement | One-day breadth bounce fades | % above 50DMA, sector breadth | Wait for persistence |
| Cross-index inconsistency | NDX strong, broader market weak | SPX, RSP, Russell breadth | Avoid over-reading one index |

### 5) Next Steps

- Track breadth across major US indices, not just one benchmark.
- Compare breadth changes with rates, credit, and volatility regimes.
- Build alert thresholds for key breadth deterioration or thrust signals.
